## The Problem With Crypto Prop Firms

Crypto prop firms operate in a less regulated environment than forex or futures prop firms. This attracts legitimate innovation and outright scams in equal measure.

**The good:** Crypto-native prop firms offer higher leverage, crypto pairs, and USDT accounts that avoid banking restrictions in some countries.

**The bad:** Crypto prop firms can disappear overnight. There is no regulator to complain to. Your evaluation fee is gone.

**The ugly:** Some firms pay out only in their own token, which has no real value. Others have no intention of paying at all. They collect fees and vanish.

## Red Flags: How To Spot A Crypto Prop Firm Scam

### 1. Pays In Its Own Token

If a firm pays out profits in its own cryptocurrency token, that token is probably worthless. You cannot sell it, or selling it crashes the price. Legitimate firms pay in USDT or fiat.

**Rule:** If they created the coin they pay with, do not trade there.

### 2. No Track Record

If the firm launched in 2025 or 2026 and has no verifiable payout history, be skeptical. Anybody can build a website. Few can run a sustainable prop firm.

**Rule:** Check for published payout reports. If none exist, assume they do not pay.

### 3. Unrealistic Profit Promises

If a firm says you can make 50% in a week or guarantees returns, it is a scam. Legitimate prop firms do not promise profits. They provide capital and take a share of what you earn.

**Rule:** If it sounds too good to be true, it is a scam.

### 4. No Real Address Or Registration

Legitimate firms have a registered business address, license numbers, and real people behind them. Crypto scam firms often list a PO box or nothing at all.

**Rule:** Verify the company registration. If you cannot find it, do not deposit.

### 5. All Positive Reviews

A legitimate firm will have mixed reviews. Some people will complain about slow payouts or strict rules. A firm with only 5-star reviews across all platforms is probably astroturfing.

**Rule:** Check Trustpilot and Forex Peace Army for real trader experiences.

## Red Flag Checklist

- [ ] Firm pays in its own token
- [ ] Firm launched in 2025 or 2026
- [ ] No verifiable payout reports
- [ ] Promises unrealistic returns
- [ ] No registered business address
- [ ] Only positive reviews
- [ ] No real people visible (LinkedIn, team page)
- [ ] Vague or missing terms and conditions
- [ ] Reports of withdrawal issues on forums

If any three of these apply, avoid the firm.
